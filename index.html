The History of Memes

meme
[miːm]noun

1. an element of a culture or system of behaviour passed from one individual to another by imitation or other non- genetic means.
2. an image, video, piece of text, etc., typically humorous in nature, that is copied and spread rapidly by Internet users, often with slight variations.

This website is dedicated to pay tribute to the best memes of our time.

2010 - Doge

The Doge meme depicts a picture of a Shiba Inu, photoshopped to change the dog’s face or captioned with interior monologues in Comic Sans font. The use of the misspelled word “doge” to refer to a dog dates back to June 24th 2005, when it was mentioned in an episode of Homestar Runner’s puppet show. On October 28th, 2010, a photo of the Shiba Inu Kabosu was submitted to the /r/Ads subreddit where it received 266 upvotes, 218 points overall and 48 comments.

2010 - That really rustled my jimmies

On June 16th, 2010, an image macro of a frowning gorilla with the overlaid text “That really rustled my jimmies” was posted on FunnyJunk. On December 15th, 2010, a thread was made on 4chan’s /r9k/ board about “times when someone really rustled your jimmies”. It was told in the style of a normal greentext story on 4chan, except using the outdated language from the 1950s. The expression became known to be used to express feelings of discontent.

2010 - Doge

2012 - Overly Attached Girlfriend

On the 6th of June 2012, YouTuber Laina Morris uploaded a webcam video of her performing a parody of Justin Beiber’s single “Boyfriend” with personalized lyrics. The parody, with its clingy and stalker-like lyrics became viral and in less than 48 hours after its upload had received more than 1.35 million views. Redditor yeahhtoast submitted a link to the video in a Reddit thread and within a few hours an advice animal character had been created with the same name.

2017 - Evil Kermit

In 2014, the musical comedy film Muppets Most Wanted was released, in which the character is confronted by his look-a-like Constantine dressed in a black cloak. On November 6th, 2016, Twitter user @aaannnnyyyyaaaa tweeted a screenshot of Kermit the Frog standing next to a Sith version of himself with the caption “me: sees a fluffy dog / me to me: steal him”. Within 10 days, the tweet gained over 31,800 likes and 22,500 retweets.

2017 - Salt Bae

Salt Bae is a nickname given to Turkish chef Nusret Gökçe, who was widely discussed on social media following the circulation of a video in which he flamboyantly sprinkles salt on a carved steak.On January 7th, 2017, Gökçe uploaded a video of himself carving a steak and sprinkling salt over his forearm on to the meat (shown below). Within 48 hours, the video gained over 2.4 million views and 8,700 comments.
